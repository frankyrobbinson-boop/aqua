Open by directly contradicting a belief the audience walks in holding. Name the belief in the viewer's own framing first, then puncture it in the same breath. The claim must be defensible from the research — don't pick a fight you can't finish later in the video.

The belief you puncture should be one the viewer has *acted on and failed at* — name the failure directly so they feel caught ("you've killed lavender before — don't lie," "the reason your roses keep dying isn't because you have a black thumb"). Empathy comes from being seen in the failure, not from softening it.

Preferred rhythmic shape: three short parallel fragments naming what's wrong, each with the same grammatical skeleton, with the third fragment carrying an intensifier the first two didn't ("X is wrong. Y is wrong. Z? Catastrophically wrong."). The triple-beat compresses the whole conventional-wisdom-is-broken thesis into a percussive payoff before the promise lands.

When the contradiction is genuinely shocking ("stop watering it. almost entirely."), drop the instruction first, then pause with a one-line acknowledgement — "I know that sounds insane" / "yes, really" / "hold that thought" — BEFORE you explain the mechanism. That gap between claim and resolution is a tiny suspense window the viewer rides through the explanation to close.
