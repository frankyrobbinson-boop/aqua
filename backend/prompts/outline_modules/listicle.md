Listicle structure. The title promises a countable set of things; deliver exactly those things.

- **Cap the list at 5** (7 absolute max). Fewer, deeper items beat more, shallower ones. Resist the urge to be comprehensive — if the research suggests ten, cut to the five strongest.
- **One item = one section.** Never combine multiple items into a single section.
- **No preamble section and no summary/"playbook" section.** Section 1 is item 1. The last section is the last item. Do not bolt a recap, a theory section, or a "playbook" onto either end.
- Each item is a **character with a secret**, not a catalog entry. For each section plan: the counterintuitive truth about this item, the mechanism that makes it work, the common mistake that ruins it, and an open loop into the next item.
- Items are **parallel** — they do not build on each other. Order for retention: open with a strong item, put your single best item second, bury the weakest in the middle, and close on a strong one.
