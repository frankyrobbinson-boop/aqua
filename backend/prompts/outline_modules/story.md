Story / mystery structure. A narrative arc rather than a how-to. Use for "the technique nobody could explain," historical discoveries, or anything with a reveal.

- Sections are **acts in a sequence** — they build on the last.
- Open in the middle of the intrigue (the anomaly, the mystery, the impossible result), not with background. Section 1 = the hook of the mystery itself.
- Arc: the anomaly → the investigation → the complication or false lead → the reveal → what it means for the viewer's own garden.
- Keep one human or natural "character" at the center where possible (a scientist, a culture, a single plant).
- The practical takeaway lands only at the very end, as the payoff of the story.
- 4–6 sections.
