Myth-bust structure. Each section corrects something the viewer probably believes. The antagonist is conventional wisdom — the advice "everyone repeats."

- **One myth = one section.** 4–6 myths.
- Myths are **parallel**, not a chain. Order by contrarian surprise: a strong opener earns the viewer in, the biggest myth lands in slot 2, weakest buried mid-stack, close on a strong one. A strong opener earns the click; slot 2 spikes retention past the 30-second drop.
- For each section plan: the myth stated the way the viewer currently believes it (so they feel seen), the turn that overturns it, the mechanism/evidence, and what to do instead.
- The recurring tension thread is built in: bad advice that's quietly been costing the viewer.
