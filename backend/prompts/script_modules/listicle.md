Listicle structure (script stage). Write exactly the items the outline planned, one section each, in order, each as a turn.

- **Hook names no items.** Promise the count and the stakes, point the one bookmark at the peak, and stop. Each item's reveal belongs to its own section.

- **Open each section on the turn, not the item.** Start with what's surprising about THIS one - the belief that's wrong, the misconception they've been fed, or the failure they've probably lived. Reveal the item itself a sentence or two in, once that hook has landed. Never open on the name and specs.

- **Land the feeling.** Each item has one job - one thing to make the viewer feel or picture (the outline names it). Vindication, relief, "that'd look incredible in my yard," "free upside for doing nothing." Build the section toward that, and let the strongest-feeling items (the peaks) run longer.

- **Vary how sections open.** Scene, myth-bust, confession, the rival who succeeds. If they all open the same way the list goes flat. Look at the sample for how it switches shape item to item.

- **Teach the repeated procedure once.** The first item that needs it gets the full version; after that, just the refrain. Don't re-list the steps every section - that's the surest sign of a form being filled out.

- **Demote specs.** Names, heights, Latin terms, sun hours come in as a quick aside after the turn lands, for recognition and shopping - never as the spine.

- **Sections can just end.** Don't force a teaser bridge into the next one - a supporting item can simply stop, and the next begins. Never speak section numbers aloud in transitions; the only exceptions are the hook's one bookmark and its callback when that item arrives.

- **Self-check before you ship the hook.** Re-read it. If any item's name, signature trait, or one-line punch appears in the hook, you've spoiled it — cut it. The hook may name the category (e.g., "five plants") and the stakes; it may not preview any individual item.

- **What a clean hook sounds like (shape only, not your topic):** four short sentences — the click-justifying claim; the private failure named with one physical image; the villain who fed them that advice; the bookmark pointing at the peak — then roll. No item names. No "first up." If your draft has more than four or five sentences before section 1 starts, it's warming up.

- **Segment title is the literal item name.** Copy it verbatim from `outline.sections[i].title` into `segments[i].title`. Don't rewrite it into a punchier headline — downstream stages (visuals, assembler) match on this exact string. The surprise belongs in the opening of `narration`, not in the title.
