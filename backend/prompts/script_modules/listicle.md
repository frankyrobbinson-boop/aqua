- **One segment per item, in the outline's order.**
- **The hook may promise the count and the stakes, but must NOT name or list the items.** Each item's identity is a reveal owned by its own section. "Five free fertilizers your kitchen makes nightly" — never "five free fertilizers: molasses, banana peels, rice water…"
- **Each item-section runs 6–8 beats, roughly in this order:**
  1. **Open with a question, a counterintuitive claim, or "here's what nobody mentions."** Never open with a definition or with the item's name.
  2. **Reveal the item 1–2 sentences in.** The reveal is a small payoff, not a header.
  3. **Mechanism.** Why this works. Sensory detail (color, texture, smell, sound, weight).
  4. **Authority anchor — OPTIONAL, OPPORTUNISTIC.** Only if the research has a real named figure with year + institution + consequence. Skip entirely otherwise. NEVER invent or stretch one. Many segments will not have this beat, and that's correct.
  5. **Analogy or metaphor — OPTIONAL.** A physical picture the viewer can hold ("a tea bag releasing color," "a dinner bell for microbes"). Skip if no good one comes — never force.
  6. **Mistake-as-cliffhanger.** Tease the mistake BEFORE delivering it ("the mistake that kills more X than any other," "where almost everyone goes wrong — and this one stings"). The mistake gets its own mini-loop, not housekeeping at the end.
  7. **Open loop into the next item** — framed as revelation or stake, not logistics ("this one feeds the plant — but the next doesn't feed the plant at all…").
- **Beats 4 and 5 are opportunistic — no quota.** Use when relevant and impactful; skip otherwise. A clean 6-beat section beats a padded 8-beat one.
- **Vary the opening move across items** — a question, a "what if," a myth, a tiny story — so the segments don't share an obvious template.
- Do not write a recap or "playbook" segment. The last item is the last segment.
