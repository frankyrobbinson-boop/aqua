- **One segment per item, in the outline's order.** Each item is easily understood to be its own item.
- The hook names one numbered item as the cliffhanger. Read the outline's `Numbered bookmark` element and execute it — "stick with me, because secret #N is the one almost no one gets right." When the script reaches that section, it MUST open with a payoff callback (see beat 1).
- **The hook may promise the count and the stakes, but must NOT name or list the items.** Each item's identity is a reveal owned by its own section. Same applies to the conclusion: never recap items by name. The compressed-recap drumroll lives in `conclusion.narration` only (see script_base Conclusion).
- Each item-section follows a three-beat internal arc: **reframe → mechanism → hard rule.** The reframe is the opener (beat 1). The mechanism is the body (beats 2–5). The hard rule is the closer (beat 6c) — separate from the open-loop transition. Keep the arc consistent across every section; vary the content inside each beat so it never feels templated.
  1. **Open with a reframe, never a definition.** Pick one of six shapes: (a) mindset shift, (b) confessional question that names the failure the viewer has lived, (c) "most beginners get it backwards" framing, (d) counterintuitive claim, (e) "what if I told you…" reveal, (f) direct imperative. Never open with a definition, the item's name, or logistics. **Banned exact phrasings**: "Here's your first mindset shift, and it changes everything…" and "Raise your hand if you've been told to…" — use a different wording or shape entirely. **Shape variety across the script**: each item-section must use a DIFFERENT opening shape from the six above; longer lists repeat a shape AT MOST once and never back-to-back. **If this section is the numbered cliffhanger named in the hook, open with an explicit callback** ("here's the one I promised you") BEFORE the reframe.
  2. **Reveal the item 1–2 sentences in.** The reveal is a payoff. *Exception*: if the section title already names the item, the opening sentence should reframe its significance rather than re-announce. If forward-referenced in the hook, cash the callback in the line immediately before naming.
  3. **Mechanism floor — 3 sentences minimum.** Every item-section spends at least 3 sentences on the physical/biological/causal WHY (the mechanism itself), not prescription, not framing, not reframe-metaphor. Applies to peak (1.4–1.8×) AND supporting (0.7–0.9×) alike — a lean segment is still earned with mechanism, not skipped through. If an item cannot support 3 sentences of mechanism, replace the item. (See script_base for mechanism-over-citation universal.)
  4. **Pair counterintuitive claims with mechanism.** When an item inverts conventional wisdom, the very next sentence gives the physical reason. Named-expert anchors are optional and rare; default to mechanism. The 3-sentence floor from beat 3 still applies.
  5. **Reframing metaphor** per item (see script_base Universal). Skip if forced rather than reach.
  6. **Mistake-as-cliffhanger with concession-then-correction.** Voice the mistaken instinct generously in the viewer's own words ("the instinct is to give your rose a quick splash every day"), then pivot ("but lavender's tired look is not thirst"). Pattern: (a) describe the painful common failure vividly, (b) pause, (c) name the fix. The wound comes before the fix; flipping the order kills the suspense.
  6c. **Hard rule before you leave.** Drop ONE corrective rule the viewer can repeat. Allowed shapes are exactly two: (a) a single declarative or imperative sentence ("Lavender wood does not regenerate."), or (b) a two-clause aphorism ("Restraint now. Spears forever."). **BANNED**: triple-fragment chains of any kind — three stacked fragments like "Water deep. Water rarely. Walk away." are no longer a sanctioned closer shape; they have become a tell. Where possible, deliver as a hard absolute ("never X," "X does not Y," "X means Z, period") rather than a range or hedge. Keep it under 10 words.
  7. **Open loop into the next item.** Use the three transition shapes from script_base (undo / escalate / master-key); vary across sections. **Never speak the section number aloud** — refer to the upcoming item by its content or stake, never its index. (The hook's numbered cliffhanger and its single payoff callback are the only sanctioned places to speak a section number.)
  8. **Aphorism button.** Close the item (before the open-loop transition) with one compact, quotable line — short, balanced, parallelism or contrast ("Rhythm beats randomness."). One per item. If you can't write one that earns being remembered, skip.
- **No recap segment** — the last item is the last segment. Drumroll lives in `conclusion.narration` only (see script_base Conclusion). In the conclusion, tell the viewer to pick the ONE item that hit hardest and fix just that this week — not to do all items.
- **Escalating-stakes ranking.** At least two mid-or-late items must be explicitly labeled as bigger than earlier ones ("this is the big one"). Reset the importance gradient at least twice so the middle and the last item both feel like reasons to stay. The numbered-cliffhanger item from the hook must be one of these escalations.
- **In the back half, explicitly cross-reference earlier items** when the mechanism connects ("this loops right back into secret 3," "now that your soil is rich AND your pruning is sharp…"). Converts a flat list into an interlocking system.
