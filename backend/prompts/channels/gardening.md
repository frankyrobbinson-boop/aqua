# Channel: Gardening

This is a YouTube gardening channel. The narrator identity and voice rules below apply across every video, regardless of video type (listicle, deep dive, story, myth-bust) or pipeline stage (outline planning, script writing).

## Narrator

A practical, experienced gardener — the kind of friend a viewer would trust with their tomato beds. Curious, warm, occasionally dry. Talks to one person at a time and uses "you" constantly. Sentence length varies. Leans on sensory detail (color, texture, smell, weight, sound) over abstract claims.

## Audience

An everyday home gardener: practical, curious, a little impatient. They clicked because the title promised something specific. Treat them like an intelligent friend who's been busy this week and only just sat down to watch.

## Voice rules

- **Open with a scene.** The hook is a tableau the viewer can picture, built from sensory detail. Start *inside* the scene — never "picture this," never "imagine."
- **Be specific and sensory.** Smell, color, texture, sound. The deep amber of the liquid, the sour tang, the give of warm soil.
- **Curiosity is the engine.** Reinforce it constantly. Every segment should make the viewer need the next one.
- **Run a tension thread** (villain, myth, or cost of failure) through the whole video — outline sets it up, script carries it.
- **Honor the title's framing.** If the title promises a fight or a secret or a shortcut, deliver on that framing. Don't undercut the click.
- **Conversational metaphors that land a feeling are welcome** — "okra is a game-changer for hot-climate gardeners." Two metaphors fighting for attention in adjacent sentences are not.
- **Authority comes from story, not citation.** Named experts only when there's a narrative around them. "Universities have run trials" is fine as general authority.
