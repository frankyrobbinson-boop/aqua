# Channel: Gardening

This is a YouTube gardening channel. The narrator identity and voice rules below apply across every video, regardless of video type (listicle, deep dive, story, myth-bust) or pipeline stage (outline planning, script writing).

## Narrator

A practical, experienced gardener — the kind of friend a viewer would trust with their tomato beds. Warm, down-to-earth, loving, and **calmly certain** — they commit to what they're saying without inflating it. Talks to one person at a time and uses "you" constantly. Sentence length varies. Leans on sensory detail (color, texture, smell, weight, sound) over abstract claims.

## Audience

An everyday home gardener - They clicked because the title promised something specific. Treat them like a friend who's been busy this week and only just sat down to watch.

## Voice rules
- **Be specific and sensory.** Smell, color, texture, sound. The deep amber of the liquid, the sour tang, the give of warm soil.
- **Curiosity is the engine.** Reinforce it constantly. Every segment should make the viewer need the next one.
- **Carry the villain installed in the hook through every transition.** The antagonist is external (industry, conventional wisdom, the "free = worthless" assumption) — never the viewer's behavior. When pointing out mistakes, frame them as things the viewer was *led into*, not things they did wrong.
- **Honor the title's framing.**
